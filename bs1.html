HISTORY

Lambs and Tigers is a strategic, two-player board game originated in Tamil Nadu(Aadu Puli Aattam) and is also played in Karnataka(Huli gatta) and Andhra Pradesh(Puli Joodam or Puli - Meka). The game is asymmetric in that one player controls three tigers and the other player controls up to 15 goats. The tigers 'hunt' the goats while the goats attempt to block the tigers' movements. In ancient days, we can see the engravings of the game chart in every veranda.

How to play

The game starts with three tigers, one placed at the apex, and the other two placed in the boxes next to it. All goats start off the board. The coins should be placed at the intersections of the board lines. The player controlling the goat starts first by placing a goat coin at any free intersection points on the board. Then it is the tiger's turn. One of the tigers is moved to its adjacent intersection point. Moves alternate between players. A tiger captures a goat by jumping over to an adjacent free position. Goat coins can't move until all the 15 are placed on the board.
